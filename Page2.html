🌐 HTML : Hyper Text Markup Language

    HTML stands for HyperText Markup Language.
    It is the backbone of every website — the standard language we use to structure and organize content on the web.

    👉 Break it down:

    • HyperText → Think of it like “super text” that can connect to other pages using links. That’s what makes the web a “web.”
    • Markup Language → We “mark up” normal text with tags to give it meaning and structure.

🏷️ Headings <h1> to <h6>

    Headings are like titles and subtitles. They create a clear hierarchy for your webpage. This helps both humans and machines (like search engines) understand the structure of your content. 

            
    Main Title
    
        
    Section Title
    
        
    Sub-section
    
        
    Smaller Heading
    
        
    Even Smaller
    
        
    Tiny Heading
    • <h1> → The most important heading, usually used once per page.
    • <h2> → Major section titles.
    • <h3> → Sub-sections under <h2>.
    • ...all the way down to <h6>, the least important heading.

    💡 Don’t just use headings to make text big — they carry meaning and importance.

📄 Paragraphs <p>

    The <p> tag is the most common tag you’ll use. It groups sentences together into paragraphs and browsers automatically add spacing around them. 

            
    This is the first paragraph. It introduces a topic.
    
        
    This is another paragraph. It is separate from the first one.

    💡 Use multiple <p> tags instead of hitting “Enter” many times.

➖ Horizontal Rule <hr>

    The <hr> tag creates a horizontal line to separate sections. It’s like drawing a divider on your page. 

            
    Topic one ends here.
    
        
    
        
    A new topic begins here.

↩️ Line Break <br>

    A line break is like pressing Enter once. It’s useful for addresses, poems, or short text that shouldn’t be a full paragraph. 

                    
    221B Baker Street
    London, England

🔗 Anchor Tag <a>

    The <a> tag creates hyperlinks. This is what makes the web “clickable” and lets us move between pages. 

            Visit Coder Army
    • href → The destination link.
    • target="_blank" → Opens the link in a new tab.

🖼️ Image Tag <img>

    The <img> tag embeds an image into your webpage. It’s a self-closing tag and needs at least two attributes:

    • src → The image file path or URL.
    • alt → Alternative text, used by screen readers and shown if the image doesn’t load.

    💡 Always use alt text for accessibility and SEO.
